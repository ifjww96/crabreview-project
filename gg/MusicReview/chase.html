Chase Atlantic's self-titled debut album, released in 2017, is a dynamic blend of alternative R&B, indie rock, and electronic pop. The album shows the band's unique ability to combine these genres into a smooth, seductive sound that keeps listeners intrigued from start to finish.


The opening track, “Into It” immediately establishes the album’s laid-back yet seductive tone. The combination of smooth vocals and infectious beats pulls listeners in, setting the stage for the emotional rollercoaster that follows. The song explores the intensity of infatuation, with lyrics that balance playful flirtation with vulnerability. It’s the perfect introduction to the band's sound, effortlessly blending genre influences into a track that’s both fresh and familiar.

Moving into “Cassie,” the band shifts to a more melancholic, yet melodic, tone. This track stands out as one of the most emotionally charged songs on the album, with its poignant lyrics about a complicated relationship. The juxtaposition of tender, introspective lyrics and the song’s infectious, upbeat rhythm creates a contrast that is both captivating and deeply emotional. It’s a track that highlights the band’s strength in storytelling, blending personal experiences with fiction in a way that resonates with listeners on a personal level.

“The Walls” brings a darker energy to the album. With its heavy beats, atmospheric synths, and introspective lyrics, the song feels like a deep dive into the feeling of being trapped in one’s own mind, yearning for freedom. The music mirrors this sense of confinement, with its deep, throbbing bass and eerie ambiance. It’s a powerful moment on the album, showcasing the band's ability to create intense emotional landscapes through sound.

In contrast, “Dancer in the Dark” introduces a haunting, atmospheric vibe, marked by its slow tempo and introspective lyrics. This track feels like a moment of self-reflection, as the singer contemplates the search for meaning in a world of darkness and uncertainty. The eerie, ethereal production adds to the somber mood, creating a feeling of being lost in thought or wandering through a fog.

One of the highlights of the album, “Swim,” offers a smoother, more melodic sound that complements its lyrics about being submerged in emotion and experiences. The song’s layered production and seductive rhythm make it an instant earworm, with the chorus staying with you long after the song ends. It's a track that finds beauty in its simplicity, offering a respite from the more intense moments on the album.

On the other hand, “Triggered” stands out for its fast-paced energy and raw, emotional intensity. The song’s lyrics address inner turmoil and emotional conflict, with the driving beat matching the urgency and tension in the vocals. It’s a cathartic, high-energy track that serves as a jolt of adrenaline in the middle of the album.

The tracks “Ozone” and “Gravity” showcase the band's ability to experiment with different sounds and textures. Both songs create a dreamy, otherworldly atmosphere with their atmospheric synths and layered production. The lyrics are introspective, exploring themes of self-doubt and longing, but the music provides a sense of weightlessness, creating a balance between the emotional heaviness of the words and the ethereal quality of the production.

The album closes with “Angeline,” a softer, more intimate track that brings a sense of closure to the album. With its gentle guitar strums and reflective lyrics, the song touches on themes of love and loss, offering a sense of emotional resolution. It’s a fitting end to the album, leaving listeners with a lingering sense of melancholy and nostalgia.

Overall, Chase Atlantic is a highly impressive debut, one that shows the band’s ability to blend genres and explore complex emotions while maintaining a distinctive sound. Each track offers something unique, whether it’s a catchy hook, emotional depth, or atmospheric production. The band’s growth is evident throughout the album, with their ability to seamlessly mix R&B, indie rock, and electronic elements into a cohesive and captivating body of work. For fans of artists like The Weeknd, The 1975, and Troye Sivan, Chase Atlantic offers a fresh and exciting sound that is sure to stand out in the genre.
