HAVE YOU EVER SEEN THIS MAN?

THIS MAN is the debut single album by JxW, a SEVENTEEN subunit consisting of members Jeonghan and Wonwoo. Focusing on an urban legend-based concept
THIS MAN’s audiobook and “어젯밤 (Last night)”’s music video show the story of “a man who turns into a nightmare, and another man who lives in fear of being forgotten” – the single album’s three songs are connected through this story and its characters..


The title track, “Last Night,” is a smooth, seductive offering that sets the tone for the rest of the album. The song's production is sleek, with plucked guitar lines and a chunky bass that drives the sound forward. However, instead of relying on a traditional, explosive chorus, the track loops a catchy synth riff throughout. This approach recalls a style SEVENTEEN was particularly fond of around 2017, though this structure is now a bit of a forgotten art. While the track doesn’t feel entirely new, it brings enough nostalgia to feel fresh. Jeonghan and Wonwoo’s contrasting vocal tones are a highlight of the song, with Jeonghan’s high, airy falsetto juxtaposed against Wonwoo’s deeper, smoother voice. However, despite the appealing vocal chemistry, the song doesn’t give them much to work with. The lyrics and melodies remain minimal, with much of the track feeling whispered rather than sung. This creates an atmospheric mood but leaves the song feeling incomplete. The producers clearly prioritized mood over structure, which works to a degree but ultimately leaves the track feeling like a tease. The song loops its few tricks, never reaching a true climactic point, and as a result, the payoff is underwhelming. The other tracks on the album, including “Beautiful Monster” and “Leftover,” continue to play with mood and atmosphere but lack the same punch. “Beautiful Monster” has a darker edge, with its deep, brooding lyrics, while “Leftover” leans into introspection, giving both Jeonghan and Wonwoo a chance to showcase their individual styles. However, neither track fully develops their potential, and much like “Last Night,” they leave listeners wanting more. Despite the album's minimalism, there’s an undeniable charm in its simplicity. The atmosphere is captivating, and the conceptual tie between the songs is intriguing, even if it doesn't reach the full potential it promises. While Last Night doesn’t fully deliver on the emotional tension it sets up, it remains an interesting start for JXW, with room for growth in future releases.
